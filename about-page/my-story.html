My Story So Far...

My college, career, and creative journey.

How I learned to code.

I first learned how to code using Microsoft Small Basic for our computer literacy course in 9th grade. I made simple programs like a BMI calculator, autobiography maker, and bootleg Tetris. I was compelled with how I was able to manipulate the computer and create interesting things with it.

When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, I was stuck at home -- like everybody else. Being bored and essentially losing my social life, I took the responsive web development course of FreeCodeCamp.org and received my certification. It was a fun learning experience and helped me create the foundation of my frontend development and design skills.

In my senior year of high school, being a STEM major, our final major requirement was for us to create a research study on mathematics and create a mobile application. We created the app using the MIT App Inventor, which was a step-back from my text-based code experience, however taught me some advanced topics like backend, databases and user authentication.

Entering college in 2022, I learned Java and the advanced computing concepts that essentially shaped me to be capable of writing full stack code; essentially the software engineer that I am today. Obviously, I couldn't tell much about it since I am still pursuing my undergraduate studies. Check back soon for more.

How design found me.

Before dreaming of becoming a software engineer, I was very much inclined to becoming an architect. As a kid, around aged 10-12, I loved to draw these grand buildings and houses with colonial architecture. I thought it was set-in-stone, but everything changed when I was finally allowed to use my mom's desktop PC. The only thing I kept playing besides Plants vs Zombies was Minecraft. This was the 2010s, so Minecraft was pretty simple and barebones. Even then, I turned my architecture illustrations to reality in Minecraft.

Then, I found my groove and style in designing when I explored Adobe Photoshop in 6th grade. Essentially, my introduction to graphic designing. I wanted to design a logo for my GTA 5 Online crew. From then on, I started to design my own logos for fun. Entering high school, I would create my own templates for infographics, illustrations, and magazines.

Some of my relatives and friends took interest in my work, and hired me to do a few commissions such as logos, infographics, and social media publication materials for their businesses and entertainment. I became a freelance graphic designer as it was a fun and formative experience as I could see my designs become better and better. I took design seriously when a famous international Cafe, called Yi Fang, contacted me to create a menu for their Baguio branch's Instagram page. I took more commissions before joining the Children's International Summer Village Baguio Chapter as a member of the digital creatives group where I contributed in creating pubmats for our social media platforms. Then, I stopped taking client works when I got more serious in coding.

Entering college, we had plenty of group projects in creating computer-based information systems. I often led the frontend development team where I would be designing and implementing the UI/UX system. Often hard-coding using the Java Swing library, it honed my careful use of layouts and content managers which I incorporate into my work to this day. Besides the coding aspect, my design skills deepened upon taking our CS 131 Software Modeling and Analysis course where I essentially learned how to conduct requirements analysis and elicitation, software design, software architecture and prototyping.

How I fell in love with filmmaking.

I made my first short film in 7th grade for a course project. I was the director and editor. Being my first film, it was messy and unpredictable. However, it taught me the basics and essentials.

At the time, I was using Sony Vegas 6. To enhance my editing skills, I created a YouTube channel where I started to create gaming videos on Team Fortress 2, League of Legends, and a bit of Call of Duty Black Ops 2. I learned basic sound editing, transitions, color grading, as well as pacing and dialogue editing. I tried transitioning to creating vlogging content during the peak of Casey Neistat and the rise of the Paul Brothers, but my content was not as well received. I stopped creating content after that to pursue other things.

When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, as I am a few months shy from completing my sophomore year, I repursued filmmaking when I volunteered to spearhead a short film in our Filipino course. Our course focused on the literary work of Dr. Jose Rizal's El Filibusterismo. I stepped out of my comfort zone by opting to use Adobe Premiere Pro where I learned advanced editing techniques and explored VFX editing using Adobe After Effects. I thoroughly enjoyed the whole process, being a major upgrade from my previous content, I eventually won as the Media Awardee for my excellent skills in digital arts and filmmaking.
